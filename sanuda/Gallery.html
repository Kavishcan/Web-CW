Humpback Whale

s The humpback whale is a large marine mammal found in oceans worldwide. They are known for their long migrations, acrobatic displays, and distinctive songs, and can grow up to 50 feet long

Clown Fish

The clownfish is a small, brightly colored fish found in tropical oceans. They have a unique relationship with sea anemones, living in their tentacles and gaining protection from predators. Popularized by the movie Finding Nemo, they are known for their playful behavior and can grow up to 4 inches long.

Octopus

The octopus is a highly intelligent and adaptable marine animal found in all of the world's oceans. With eight arms, each lined with suction cups, they are expert hunters and can camouflage themselves to blend in with their surroundings. Some species can grow up to 30 feet in length.

Jelly Fish

Jellyfish are free-swimming, gelatinous marine creatures found in all the world's oceans. They have a distinctive umbrella-shaped bell and trailing tentacles with stinging cells used for capturing prey. Some species have bioluminescent abilities, while others can grow up to 8 feet in diameter.

Orca

Orcas, also known as killer whales, are the largest members of the dolphin family and are found in oceans worldwide. They are apex predators and intelligent hunters, known for their distinctive black and white markings and social behavior. Orcas can grow up to 30 feet in length and live up to 90 years.

Star Fish

Starfish, also known as sea stars, are found in oceans around the world. They have a distinctive star-shaped body with multiple arms, which they use to capture prey and move along the ocean floor. Some species can regenerate lost limbs, while others have the ability to change color.

Sting Ray

The stingray is a flat-bodied fish found in oceans and rivers worldwide. They have a distinctive diamond-shaped body with a long, whip-like tail that has a venomous spine used for self-defense. Stingrays can grow up to 6 feet in length and are known for their graceful swimming movements.

Turtle

Turtles are shelled reptiles found in oceans, rivers, and on land around the world. They have a distinctive hard shell that provides protection, and flippers or legs for movement. Some species can live up to 100 years, and they are known for their slow, deliberate movements and migratory behavior.
